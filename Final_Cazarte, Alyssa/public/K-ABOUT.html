South Korean Fashion


May your HEART and your CLOSET be full!


Harmony and a sense of community are important traditional values in South Korea and you can see the crossing of that even in Korean fashion. That means that although there are hundreds of thousands of stores all over Korea, either physical shops or Korean online clothing stores, many of them sell the same style of clothing items. At the very least very similar ones anyway. That is also because many clothing stores, especially the smallerones, use local manufacturers which may limit the variety of the shops to choose from.



However, don’t take that to mean that everyone on the streets of South Korea wears the same outfit. While many do share the same sense of style, there are also distinct differences between these styles, apart from the Korean clothing brands they belong to. Let’s explore the different types of Korean fashion styles out there.

While it’s a very unisex look, girls do wear dresses and skirts that go well with the look, as well. Girls also typically like to mix more colors into their outfits instead of sticking to predominantly black each and every time.



As Korean Fashion Trends and the Style for Korean women evolve though you still won’t see many women wearing a backless tops or tank tops, many are in love with off-shoulder and cold shoulder tops these days. And while the chest area remains covered, crop tops have become a big trend. They’re especially popular to mix with high-waisted pants, shorts, and skirts.

On the other hand, going mini in the lower body has absolutely been a Korean fashion trend for many years, Korean women love to show their legs, they do usually wear undershorts (or the skirts come equipped with it), to protect themselves from showing a little too much. The skirts are typically either tennis skirts or bodycon skirts, in both cases, they are usually high-waisted. For shorts, jean shorts are especially popular.



From tops to coats, and pants to dresses, Korean women love wearing clothes oversized enough to hide their forms and is a typical Korean style clothing. They are especially a popular casual attire, existing even in the form of sweater dresses, and focused on utmost comfort. It also offers a cute but effortless look.

Many women love to combine an oversized item with a tighter one, usually a loose t-shirt, blouse, or sweater, with skinny jeans or a skirt. Others may wear an oversized top together with a loose bottom, which is another popular type of style gaining popularity amongst Koreans.

Plaid Blazer

Made from cotton material, have remained one of the most popular fall and winter staples, “it” fashion item of the season, and you’re going to want to get your hands on one you can wear to any occasion because they really are that versatile.

As fall goes on and the days get colder, a cosy sweater under a plaid blazer is an easy go- to for work or any casual occasion. If your blazer is more colourful, opt for a white sweater, as it will make the blazer pop while still looking professional and understated.

Most of Korean artist always wear Plaid blazers as one of airport fashion styles! they look great with almost any solid colour bottom, like pants, skirts, slim fit jeans, mini gown, or even a boot! Plus, you can wear blazers to work during the week and wear them with jeans on the weekend!

PU Leather Jacket

One of the jackets that are popular among Korean artists is the PU leather jacket. PU stands for polyurethanes is also called faux leather. The material is made from plastic and usually coast less as compared to the original. A jacket made from this material looks almost identical to genuine leather and it’s hard to tell at first sight but you’ll sure to notice the difference after a few months. Leather jackets offer a classic style. Their classic, timeless style allows you to wear your leather jacket for many years without worrying about it bacoming an outdated style.

Fitted Blazer – V Collar Neck

Type of men’s jacket, usually worn with a dress shirt, tie, and slacks. It’s like a suit coat, except it doesn’t come with a matching pair of trousers. It’s common to find them with brass buttons. “Fitted” refers to the cut of the jacket, meaning a closer, tighter fit to the body. Not tight as in too small, but less baggy. It’s most flattering on men with a lean, athletic build. Generally speaking, the blazer should follow the natural curves of your body without pulling or sagging, If you’ve got wrinkles at the sides or across the back, the blazer is probably too small in the torso. Before you choose the blazer, make sure you fitting it and get know your size.

Sherpa Lined Jacket

As we know, even its end of January but yet the cold of winter seasons still going. For those that looking for jacket to make you stay warms, more comfortable and most important, its deliver softness first and stylish look. Here it is, Sherpa lined jacket was the best choice for mild and winter seasons. “Sherpa Lining”, in the garment industry, refers to a polyester lining with a deep pile and large, fuzzy “naps” that give it a bumpy texture. The overall effect is similar to real sheep’s wool, but Sherpa lining is lighter weight, much less bulky, and faster drying. It’s also considerably cheaper and avoids any ethical concerns about animal products.

In Korea, Sherpa line jacket was really familiar among artist. It looks good on its own: sturdy, simple, and emphatically “wintery” thanks to the fuzzy lining on the collar. You never need to get fancy with a Sherpa jacket.
